# EV Charging Infrastructure — Financial Feasibility Model

A financial model and Power BI dashboard analyzing the viability of bidirectional EV charging Solar Canopies across three lot sizes, built using real public data from SDG&E, CALeVIP, and the U.S. Department of Energy.

**Project Background**   
Developed in the context of work with Center for Community Energy, a San Diego nonprofit advocating for vehicle-to-grid (V2G) technology as a solution to California's duck curve energy problem; the duck curve refers to the evening spike in energy demand as solar generation drops and people return home. CCE proposes that EV charging at workplace parking structures can store solar energy during the day and discharge it back to the grid at peak hours, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

__Research Question__   
What are the financial returns of installing solar-powered bidirectional Level 2 EV charging infrastructure in parking structures in California, and how do lot size, EV adoption rate, and V2G export pricing affect profitability?

__Models & Analysis__   
- Multi-scenario financial model: Small (50), Medium (200), and Large (500) charger lots
- 10-year profit projection with 3% annual escalation and CALeVIP rebate modeling
- NTM Forecast vs. Actuals: 12-month forecast with simulated real-world variance, demonstrating sub-5% variance discipline
- Sensitivity analysis: Net 10-year profit scenarios across combinations of EV adoption rates and V2G export rates

__Key Findings__   
Payback period of __1.8 years__ across all lot sizes after CALeVIP rebates    
10-year ROI of approximately __299%__    
_CALeVIP rebates reduce upfront capital by 55%,_ making projects highly attractive
